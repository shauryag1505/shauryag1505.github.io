Staying Physically Fit in the Age of Screens

Simple habits to protect your body and stay active while using screens.

Screentime Effects On Physical Health

Eye Strain
(Digital Eye Strain)

Symptoms-Dry eyes, blurred vision, headaches, difficulty focusing.
Why-Staring at screens for long hours without breaks reduces blink rate and strains eye muscles.

Poor Posture
(Back & Neck Pain)

Symptoms-Neck pain, back pain, shoulder stiffness.
Why-Prolonged hunching over phones, tablets, or laptops causes spinal stress.

Increased Fatigue
(Feeling of Lethargicness)

Symptoms-Feeling constantly tired or drained.
Why-Mental overload from continuous stimulation and lack of physical movement.

Improving Physical Health

Tip 1: Set Up an Ergonomic Workspace

Maintain proper posture by adjusting your chair, desk, and screen height. Your screen should be at eye level, and your back should be straight with both feet flat on the floor.

Tip 2: Schedule Outdoor Activities

Balance screen time with regular outdoor activities like jogging, cycling, or playing sports. Aim for at least 60 minutes of physical activity every day to build strength & reduce stress.

Tip 3 : Incorporate Daily Movement Breaks

Set alarms or reminders every 30-45 minutes to stand up, stretch, or walk around. Even a 5-minute break improves circulation, reduces muscle stiffness, and refreshes your body.

Tip 4 : Join Group Fitness or Sports Clubs

Participate in team sports or group workouts. Not only does this keep you active, but it also adds a social element that replaces time spent alone on screens.
