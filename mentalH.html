Strategies to reduce Stress-Anxiety

Create Screen-Free Break Times

Schedule regular breaks during your day to disconnect, stretch, and recharge your mind.

Mindful Breathing Exercises

Use deep, slow breaths to calm anxiety after long screen sessions and improve focus.

Limit Screen Use Before Bed

Power down devices at least 1 hour before sleep to reduce anxiety and improve sleep quality.

Move Your Body Regularly

Incorporate physical activities like walking, yoga, or workouts to release tension built from sitting and scrolling.

Engage in Offline Hobbies

Balance screen time with relaxing activities like reading, drawing, or spending time outdoors to clear your mind.

Symptoms of Stress-Anxiety

Sleep Disturbances

Excessive screen time disrupts natural sleep cycles, leaving teens exhausted and unfocused during the day.

Increased Irritability

Constant digital engagement can cause mood swings, frustration, and irritability over small triggers.

Difficulty Concentrating

Frequent notifications and multitasking make it hard to focus on studies, causing anxiety over falling behind.

Fear of Missing Out (FOMO)

Endless scrolling through others' lives creates pressure to keep up, fueling stress and insecurity.

Increased Anxiety

Exposure to overwhelming news, social media comparisons, and constant information can heighten anxiety levels.
